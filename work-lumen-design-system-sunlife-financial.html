Lumen Design System

Building a design system that would allow teams to create faster, consistently, and at scale.

Atomic design methodology

Context

Design teams at Sunlife were growing. The design process was outdated ‚Äî using style guides, and multiple design reviews each week. It worked, but at a cost. We wanted to give designers and developers more time solving problems rather than pushing pixels. I helped build the Lumen Design System to do just that.

My Role

  • Create a Design System from scratch
  • Develop documentation
  • Mentor designers on using the Design System
  • Manage iterations and create improvements

    • Success metrics

  • Reduce time spent on-boarding new designers
  • Starting a project becomes easier
  • Improving speed updating work
  • Less meetings for design reviews
  • Reduce inconsistencies between design and development

    • Discovery

    My starting point was collectively understand the Sunlife brand. After some conversations with the design and brand teams, it was clear that designers were out-of-sync from brand. My next step was to starting curating the component standard. This is where Atomic design methodology came in to use. Starting with atoms (i.e. colors, text styles, icons)

    Atoms (icons, colors, text styles, buttons, inputs, etc)

    Once we had the most commonly used components built, we did a beta launch to measure if our design system was actually useful to the team (i.e: faster, more efficient)

    Feedback was positive. Designers seemed to like the direction it was going in. This was exciting for me as I had never done anything like this before.

    Browser view of the Lumen Design System

    Now that we had designers actually using the Lumen Design System, there were a lot of requests for onboarding. I mentored the team 1 on 1 as well as created onboarding steps for new members in the future.

    Onboarding for designers

    After multiple iterations of the Lumen Design System, I was proud of what we had. The design system was efficient, easy to use, consistent, accessible, scalable, and most importantly, allowed the designers to focus on what's important ‚Äî solving user and business problems and building exceptional products.

    Every component is built to WCAG and AODA standards

    Results

    After the 1st launch of Lumen Design System, there were less requests for design review meetings. Designers gave positive feedback ‚Äî easier to get started and find components with usage guidelines.

    Lumen Design System allowed teams to work 10x faster

    Learnings

  • The end goal with design is to solve problems, not just have a beautiful UI.
  • Consider accessibility first, not last.
  • You are not the user. Test the product with its users and then iterate.
  • Collaborate with developers when building design components
