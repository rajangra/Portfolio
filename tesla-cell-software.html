Hi, I’m Rajan.

It's always been dream of mine to work at Tesla. As a life-long car enthusiast, and UX Designer, I feel inspired by their mission: to accelerate the world's transition to sustainable energy.

After working as a designer for over 5 years, I feel ready to take on that challenge. And if someone from Tesla sees this, I'd love to chat!

I am fascinated by the new cell technology used in Tesla's vehicles. Especially the innovations with the 4680 Cell Design.

And when I saw this: Tesla is looking for a UX Designer in their Cell Software team. I knew I had to do something about it.

But there was just one HUGE problem. I didn’t (yet) know anything about the Cell Technology or how Cell Engineers would benefit from software and a mobile app.

It's almost christmas time.

I didn’t have much time before the new year. So I spent the next week learning about how cells are made, how cell factory manufacturing lines work, and the issues engineers and operators might face throughout their day.

Note: This is within the constraints of time (1 week) and internet access only. I didn't have access to Cell Engineers and Operators to understand their true pain points, needs, and goals.

Information on the internet may be outdated (and likely is). A lot of assumptions were made due to the nature of this exercise. However, the goal was to challenge myself, learn something new, and have fun.

These are a few questions I wanted to understand:

  • What is a cell made of? How is it made? What are all these numbers (eg: 4680)?
  • What is the cell manufacturing process, and what can go wrong?
  • How might software help engineers and operators in their day-to-day?
  • How can I design for this experience as simple and clear as possible?

    • Based on my research, I understood this:

    Cell Engineers need to keep track of production numbers, while reducing inefficiencies and bottlenecks in the manufacturing process.

    Operators need reliable machines, a safe working environment, and clear communication with engineers and other operators.

    Tesla needs to make cars as quickly as possible. And to continue to be the leading battery manufacturer in the world. Optimize factory production for speed and scale. Reduce risk of recalls or legal issues. Win customers trust and loyalty. Transition the world to sustainable energy as quickly as possible.

    Customer needs are to not wait months, let alone over a year for their EV. And they want a reliable EV.
